13. 
A square shaped garden is surrounded by a uniform path of 1 m width. If the ratio of the area of the garden and that of the path is 36 : 13, then the difference in the area of the garden and that of the path is _______.
A. 184 sq. m.
B. 92 sq. m.
C. 69 sq. m.
D. 52 sq. m.
Status :  
Unanswered
Answer : B
View Solution
Let the side of the garden be S m.
=> The area of the garden = S2
The side of the combination of the garden and the path is (S+2) m.
The area = (S+2)2 - S2
=> Their ratio = S2 : (S + 2)2 - S2
Their ratio = S2 : (S + 2)2 - S2
=> 36 : 13 = S2 : (S + 2)2 - S2
=> 13 x S2 = 36[(S+2)2 - S2]
=> 49 S2 = 36 (S+2)2
=> 7S = 6 (S+2) => S = 12 m
The area of the garden = (12)2 = 144 sq.m
The area of the path = (12+2)2 - (12)2 = 196 - 144 = 52 sq.m
The required difference = 144 - 52 = 92 sq.m
